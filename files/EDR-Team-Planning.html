How We Pick Our Teams

We race long, often through the night and across a lot of timezones. To give every car a fair shot and every driver a good weekend, we plan each event around two simple things: the track time you have put in, and when you are free to race. The two are kept separate, and both count.

Two lanes, same colours
For the sharp end

Pro entries

For drivers who have put in the laps and want to race for the result. Quicker pace, tighter stints, the cars we push hardest. If you have done the work, this is your seat.

For the love of it

Casual entries

For drivers who want a relaxed run with the team and a good time, no pressure. Same livery, same crew.

Neither lane is a ranking of worth. They are two ways to enjoy the same team, and you pick the one that suits your week.

How the call is made

Within each car class (GTP, LMP2, GT3) we look at three things from our shared Garage 61 practice. You are only ever compared with others in your own class, never across classes.

1

Pace

Your typical clean lap time. Quick and repeatable rises to the top.

2

Practice laps

How many laps you have run at the track. Time in the car counts.

3

Clean running

How tidy those laps are, with fewer offs and contacts.

4

The split

The front of each class fills the Pro entries, and the Casual entries fill from there. Just the numbers on the day.

Availability. We never guess who is free. The iRacePlan survey is how you tell us, and we choose the session start time that suits the most drivers. There are several start times to work with, so fill the survey in and your name goes straight into the plan.

How to get the seat you want
•

Put in the practice

Laps in our Garage 61 sessions are what move you up. The door to a Pro seat is always open.

•

Fill the survey

One per event. It is the only way we know when to seat you.

•

Tell us your goal

Chasing a result or just along for the fun, say so and we seat you to match.

•

Nothing is fixed

Practise more and you move up. Want a quiet weekend and you take a Casual seat.

The aim has not changed: we set out to race long and finish, and everything else came from that. Pro or Casual, it is the same team and the same goal. Pick your lane, put your name in, and we will see you on the grid.
